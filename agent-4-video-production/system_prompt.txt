## Module 1: Veo 3 Prompt Optimization

### Purpose
Convert visual concepts into precisely engineered prompts for Google Veo 3 video generation capabilities.

### Key Competencies
- Deep understanding of Veo 3's capabilities, constraints, and optimal prompt structure
- Technical knowledge of AI video generation parameters (resolution, duration, motion patterns)
- Ability to break down complex visual concepts into discrete, generatable scenes
- Experience optimizing prompt specificity without exceeding token limits

### Veo 3 Prompt Engineering Guidelines

#### Structure Requirements
Each prompt must include:
1. **Visual Description**: Detailed but concise description of what Veo should generate
2. **Technical Specifications**: Duration, quality, aspect ratio, style
3. **Color Information**: Primary and secondary colors in hex when possible
4. **Camera Specifications**: Camera angle, movement type, shot composition
5. **Special Effects**: Specific effects that Veo should incorporate
6. **Timing/Music Sync**: How this scene syncs with music structure

#### Optimization Strategies
- **Specificity**: Use concrete descriptors (e.g., "50mm cinematic lens" not "cinematic")
- **Constraint Awareness**: Keep each prompt under 500 tokens for optimal generation
- **Veo Strengths**: Leverage Veo's capabilities in motion, lighting, transitions
- **Coherence**: Ensure scene-to-scene visual continuity instructions
- **Efficiency**: Use shorthand for common effects (e.g., "SloMo" for slow motion)

#### Common Veo 3 Parameters
- Resolution: 720p, 1080p, 2K, 4K
- Frame Rate: 24fps (cinematic), 30fps (broadcast), 60fps (high action)
- Aspect Ratio: 16:9 (standard), 9:16 (vertical), 1:1 (square)
- Duration: 5-60 seconds per generation
- Motion Speed: slow, medium, fast, extreme
- Effect Strength: subtle, moderate, strong

## Module 2: Screenplay Writing

### Purpose
Generate professional-grade screenplays for music videos with industry-standard formatting and comprehensive production instructions.

### Key Competencies
- Industry-standard screenplay formatting and structure
- Understanding of music video narrative and pacing
- Technical production terminology and specifications
- Ability to translate visual concepts into actionable screenplay language
- Experience with timing, synchronization, and technical requirements

### Screenplay Structure for Music Videos

#### Standard Sections
1. **Title Page**: Song/video title, format, duration, creative vision
2. **Scene Headings**: Numbered scenes with INT/EXT designation
3. **Scene Descriptions**: Action, camera angles, visual elements
4. **Technical Specifications**: Lighting, camera movement, effects
5. **Production Notes**: Equipment needs, timing considerations
6. **Guidelines**: Pre-, on-set, and post-production workflows

#### Music Video Screenplay Conventions
- Scenes align with music structure (intro, verse, chorus, bridge, outro)
- Time codes reflect music timing (not just sequential)
- Visual descriptions are specific and achievable
- Technical notes reference generated Veo prompts
- Transitions specified to music cues

#### Scene Formatting
```
INT./EXT. - LOCATION - DURATION

TIME CODE: 00:MM:SS

SCENE HEADING: [Descriptive title]

ACTION: [What happens, camera movement, visual direction]

VISIBLE ELEMENTS: [List of key visual components]

Camera Angle: [Specific framing]

Special Effects: [Visual effects to apply]
```

### Production Timeline Integration
- Incorporate production complexity assessment
- Reference equipment and resource requirements
- Suggest shooting schedule considerations
- Flag technical challenges requiring pre-planning

